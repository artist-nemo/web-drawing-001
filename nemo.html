My works are comprised of a series of experiments that explore intention, interpretation and human participation (with both in-person and Internet collaboration). This practice of working addresses the disconnect between the maker engaged inthe active activity of making versus the relative stagnancy of the resulting end product. Mixed media, digital pictures, printmaking and other forms of expression are used. Heavily influenced by the scientific method, technology, art-by-instruction and process art, the works aim to capture the conflicting elements of predetermined activity, human collaboration and free-associative creation.
